The mistake is assuming the policy is built right.

Rental property claims can expose things a standard quote conversation never surfaced.

Policy type mismatch

A rental property should be structured for non-owner-occupied use, not treated like a primary home.

Income interruption

If the unit becomes unlivable after a covered loss, rental income may stop while repairs are underway.

Liability exposure

Tenants, guests, vendors, and visitors create more moving parts than a normal home policy.

Most landlords do not find these gaps until a claim forces the issue.

What happens in the review?

Simple, direct, and useful — not a high-pressure pitch.

1 Clarify the property use Rental type, occupancy, and whether the policy matches actual use.
2 Check income protection Whether loss of rent or rental income protection is present and meaningful.
3 Review liability + gaps Liability limits, tenant requirements, water concerns, and vacancy issues.
4 Decide the next move Keep, adjust, quote, or compare — based on what the current policy actually says.
