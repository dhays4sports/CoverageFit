Many owners confuse “having a policy” with having a resilient income property.

A commercial apartment policy should be reviewed against the property’s real risk profile: building age, unit count, tenant exposure, rental income, ordinance issues, and how long recovery could realistically take.

Assumption: “The building is insured, so I’m covered.”
Reality: Claim recovery can expose gaps around income, limits, exclusions, deductibles, and ordinance upgrades.
Better question: Could one major loss destabilize the asset’s cash flow?

Why this matters now.

Apartment owners do not need another random quote. They need to know whether the policy structure can survive a real claim.

Underwriting is tighter, and some buildings are harder to place or renew.
The worst time to discover a policy mismatch is after a major claim.
