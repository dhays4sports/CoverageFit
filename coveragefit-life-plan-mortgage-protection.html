The question is not just “do I have life insurance?”

The real question is whether your family could keep the home, cover obligations, and make decisions from stability instead of panic.

1
What protects the home? Estimate whether the mortgage, rent, or housing costs would stay manageable.
2

3
What type fits? Clarify whether term, permanent, or a blended approach makes the most sense.

The mistake is assuming the home is protected because the house is insured.

Home insurance protects the structure. Family protection planning looks at the people paying for it, the income behind it, and the obligations that continue if life suddenly changes.

The mortgage is exposed

If income stops, the house payment may become the first major pressure point.

The amount is guessed

A policy can exist and still be too small to protect income, housing, debt, and time.

The policy has no clear job

Term, whole life, and permanent coverage can all make sense — but only when the purpose is clear.

Most people are not unprotected because they do not care.

They are unprotected because nobody connected the policy to the home, income, and people depending on them.

What happens in the 10-minute plan?

This is designed to be simple, direct, and useful — not a high-pressure pitch.

1 Clarify the home risk Mortgage, rent, housing costs, and whether the home could stay stable.
2 Map who depends on you Spouse, kids, parents, business obligations, or anyone relying on your income.
3 Estimate the protection window How long your family may need support: 5, 10, 20, or 30 years.
4 Choose the policy job Decide whether the need is temporary, permanent, or a combination.

Quick questions

Is this a quote?

No. This is the planning step before a quote. The goal is to understand the right amount, type, and time horizon first.

Is this only for homeowners?

No. The home/mortgage frame is powerful for homeowners, but the same planning logic applies to rent, income, debt, children, and family stability.

Is term life or whole life better?

Neither is automatically better. The right answer depends on the job the policy needs to do.

What if I already have a policy?

Perfect. We can use it as a starting point and see whether it still matches your real life today.
