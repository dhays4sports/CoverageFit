Why I built this

People usually care about coverage. They just were never shown where the gaps hide.

After reviewing policies and seeing how coverage gaps can show up during real claims, the same pattern kept appearing: someone had insurance, but still was not fully sure whether it matched their actual situation.

CoverageFit was built to help surface those questions before the pressure of a claim, lender deadline, rental issue, or family emergency forces the answer.

Most people are not trying to buy the cheapest policy. They are trying to avoid expensive surprises later.

Why CoverageFit exists

Most people do not discover insurance problems when they buy the policy. They discover them when something actually happens.

1
Policies can look fine

A declarations page may appear complete while still leaving uncertainty around limits, exclusions, occupancy, or how the policy is actually structured.

2
Real life changes

Rentals, families, debt, income, housing, and liability exposures evolve over time. Coverage should evolve with reality.

3
Claims are stressful

These are the same types of questions that commonly show up during claims — CoverageFit is designed to surface them earlier.

What I focus on

The biggest issue usually is not whether you have insurance.

The bigger question is whether the policy is built for the job it is supposed to do. A rental property, a family protection plan, and a primary home all need to be reviewed differently.

  • Does the policy match the actual use?
  • Are the limits aligned with the real exposure?
  • Are income, housing, liability, or family stability properly considered?
  • Are there gaps that may only become obvious during a claim?
The claim-time problem

The wrong time to discover a gap is after something happens.

CoverageFit is designed around the uncomfortable but important questions people often avoid until it is too late.

If a rental becomes unlivable, does the income stop without protection?
If a tenant, guest, or vendor is hurt, are the liability limits enough?
If income disappears, does the family have time — or immediate financial pressure?
