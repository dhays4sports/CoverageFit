The goal is not just “having a landlord policy.”

The goal is making sure the policy actually matches the rental property, income stream, and liability exposure.

1
Is it structured right?Confirm the property is insured for rental use, not owner-occupied use.
2
Is the income protected?Review whether loss of rent may apply if a covered claim makes the property unlivable.
3
Are the exposures aligned?Check liability, water damage, vacancy, tenant insurance, and umbrella compatibility.

The mistake is assuming the policy matches the property.

Rental coverage can look fine on the surface while still having weak spots around use, income, tenant exposure, or claim limitations.

Wrong policy structure

A rental should not be treated like a primary home. The occupancy/use mismatch is one of the first things to check.

Income not protected

If rent stops after a covered loss, the policy needs to be reviewed for loss-of-rent or rental income protection.

Liability too low

Tenants, guests, vendors, and visitors create a different liability picture than a normal residence.

Most rental owners are not careless. They are under-reviewed.

The issue is usually that nobody slowed down and checked whether the coverage matches the way the property is actually used.
